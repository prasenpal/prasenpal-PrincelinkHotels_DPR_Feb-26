Section 02

Project Background & Strategic Rationale

A structural hospitality supply gap in a growing district headquarters, combined with a promoter possessing deep construction expertise, creates a rare and compelling project opportunity.

The Market Gap

Bankura is a district headquarters with a population exceeding 40 lakh, significant tourism assets (Bishnupur temples on UNESCO tentative list, Mukutmanipur dam, Susunia hills), and a functioning administrative hub generating consistent business travel. Yet, the district has zero branded or quality 3-star equivalent hotels.

Existing accommodation consists of small, unbranded lodges with 10‚Äì25 rooms, averaging below 3.5/5 on OTA ratings ‚Äî lacking centralized AC, elevator access, modern amenities, conference facilities, or banquet halls.

West Bengal tourist arrivals surged from 8.4 crore (2019) to 18.5 crore (2024) ‚Äî a ~17% CAGR ‚Äî with the state government specifically targeting Bankura for tourism promotion.
